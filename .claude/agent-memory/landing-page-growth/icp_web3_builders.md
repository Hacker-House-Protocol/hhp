## ICP Profile: Crypto-Native Builder

**Demographics:** 22–38 years old. Global (strong presence in LatAm, Europe, Southeast Asia, US). Often nomadic or event-circuit itinerant. Attends hackathons (ETH Global, Devcon, ETHDenver, etc.).

**Roles:** Solo devs, founders, designers, smart contract engineers, protocol strategists. Often wears multiple hats. Self-identifies as "builder" not "developer."

**Context:** Plugged into crypto Twitter/X, Farcaster, Telegram. Discovers things through peer signal, not ads. Deeply skeptical of corporate tone and marketing speak.

**Core pain points:**
1. Finding co-founders / teammates with aligned vision AND real execution skills — the hardest problem in the space
2. Hackathon teams formed in 48 hours with strangers — poor fit, zero chemistry, high drop-off post-event
3. No structured way to go from "I have an idea" to "I have a team" — the gap between ideation and building is brutal
4. IRL coordination around events is fragmented (Discord, Telegram, Twitter threads, word of mouth)
5. Short-term co-living during events is expensive, unorganized, and not builder-optimized

**Motivations (deep):**
- Ship something that matters before the next bull cycle ends
- Find the co-founder they've been looking for for 2 years
- Be part of a serious team, not a hackathon team
- Get access to IRL builder density — the magic happens in person

**JTBD (Jobs To Be Done):**
- "Help me find 1-2 builders who can execute on my idea before ETH Global"
- "Help me find a project worth joining that matches my skills"
- "Help me coordinate where to stay and who to build with during [event]"

**Key objections:**
1. "Another tool I'll sign up for and never use"
2. "Will there actually be real builders on this?"
3. "I don't want to give my email/wallet to something that isn't live yet"
4. "This is just a job board with a Web3 skin"

**What disarms objections:**
- Peer credibility (IRL event presence, community signals)
- Specificity of the archetype system — feels like it actually understands builders
- Free + no spam promise
- Clear, non-corporate voice that sounds like it was written by a builder
